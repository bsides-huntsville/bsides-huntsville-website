About

BSides Huntsville 2027 is a community-driven information security conference built by and for the local and regional security, hacker, maker, and technology communities.

BSides is about participation, not passive attendance. Participants are encouraged to share knowledge, ask questions, build things, break things, and learn from one another in an inclusive, welcoming environment.

Content

Talks, workshops, demos, and hands-on sessions ranging from beginner-friendly to deeply technical.

Conversation

Meet local builders, defenders, researchers, and students — everyone is welcome.

Community

Strengthen the local security community through learning, networking, and shared projects.

BSides Huntsville is an independent, community-organized event operating under the global BSides framework. While sponsors support the event financially, they do not influence content, speaker selection, or conference operations.

Call for Presenters (CFP)

BSides Huntsville is community content. That means everyone is welcome to submit: talks, workshops, hands-on labs, live demos, and village / interactive sessions.

We run a fair and open CFP. Proposals are reviewed based on educational value, clarity, and relevance. No speaking slots are sold — sponsors and non-sponsors use the same CFP process.

First-time speaker? We’ve got you. If you want feedback on your abstract or help shaping a workshop, the organizing team will help.

Sponsors

Sponsors help keep BSides accessible and community-driven. BSides Huntsville is not a lead-generation event.

Code of Conduct

BSides Huntsville is committed to a safe, inclusive environment for all participants. Harassment, discrimination, and abusive behavior are not tolerated.

Be excellent to each other

  • Respect boundaries and consent.
  • Help create an inclusive and welcoming environment.
  • Report concerns to organizers promptly.

Full Code of Conduct and reporting contacts will be posted before the event.
